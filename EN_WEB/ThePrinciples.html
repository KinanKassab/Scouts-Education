Definition of the Scout Movement

The Scout Movement is an educational, voluntary, non-political movement open to all without distinction of origin, race, or creed, in accordance with the purpose, principles, and method expressed by its founder.

Objective of the Scout Movement

The objective of the Scout Movement is to contribute to the education and development of young people to achieve the fullest development of their spiritual, intellectual, social, and physical capacities as individuals and as responsible citizens in their local, national, and international communities.

The Scout Promise

I promise on my honor that I will do my best:

To do my duty to God and my country.

To help other people at all times.

To obey the Scout Law.

Scout Laws

1. A Scout's honor is to be trusted.

2. A Scout is loyal.

3. A Scout's duty is to be useful and to help others.

4. A Scout is a friend to all and a brother to every other Scout.

5. A Scout is courteous.

6. A Scout is a friend to animals and loves nature.

7. A Scout obeys orders without question.

8. A Scout smiles and whistles under all difficulties.

9. A Scout is thrifty.

10. A Scout is clean in thought, word, and deed.

The Principles
