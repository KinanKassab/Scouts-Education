Lord Baden Powell and the History of Scouting

1857: Birth of Lord Robert Stevenson Smyth Baden-Powell on February 22

1876: Lord Baden-Powell joined the British Army

1876: Commissioned as a Captain in the British Army

1899: Published a book about scouting in the British Army

1907: Led a experimental camp with 20 boys

1907: Founded Boy Scouts by Lord Baden-Powell

1907: Established the first Scout troop on March 1

1909: First scout rally in London attracted over 11,000 scouts

1910: Initiated scouting program for girls under the guidance of his sister Agnes

1912: Scouting started in Syria and Lebanon

1912: Married Olave Soames

1912: Scouting started in Palestine

1913: Olave gave birth to their first child, Peter

1914: Organized scouting movement to ensure stability during World War I

1914: Established Rover Scouts

1914: Scouting started in Egypt

1915: Olave gave birth to their second child, Heather

1916: Scouting started in Sudan

1917: Olave gave birth to their third child, Betty

1918: Formation of Rover Scouts for young adults

1918: Scouting started in Iraq

1920: First World Scout Jamboree with 8,000 scouts from 34 countries

1920: Lord Baden-Powell declared Chief Scout of the world

1926: Formation of Scout Handicapped

1927: Scouting started in Jordan

1928: Formation of Sea Scouts

1930: Formation of Rover Scout Crews, and Olave becomes World Chief Guide

1941: Death of Lord Robert Stevenson Smyth Baden-Powell

1942: Scouting started in Saudi Arabia

1954: Scouting started in Libya

1977: Scouting started in United Arab Emirates
